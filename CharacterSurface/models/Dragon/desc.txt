The dragon coils through old stories as a guardian of thresholds, hoarding not merely gold but the memory of those who dared approach. Its scales catch firelight like a thousand small mirrors, each one a kept secret. To the ancients the dragon was the shape of the sky itself when it grew angry, the long body of storm and river given teeth and a will. It sleeps for centuries and wakes hungry, and the brave who seek it learn that the true treasure was never the gold at all, but the courage spent on the road to find it.
